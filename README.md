# UNP_PROJECT_BI_WITH_POWER_BI_HR_DATA_ANALYSIS

I completed this Power BI project in colebration with UNP, where I analyzes a total workforce of 1,470 employees who maintain an average age of 36.92 years and an average tenure of 7.01 years. The organization currently faces an attrition rate of 16%, totaling 237 employees, despite moderate satisfaction scores including an average Job Satisfaction of 2.73 and an Environment Satisfaction of 2.72. Demographic data reveals that the workforce is 60% male, and the highest concentration of employees—and the highest attrition (116)—falls within the 26-35 age group. Structurally, the Research & Development department is the largest with 961 employees, yet the Sales department accounts for the highest departmental attrition count at 133 employees (56.12%). Financial analysis shows that employees in lower salary slabs are more likely to leave, while the overall average monthly income stands at $6.50K. A significant driver of turnover is overtime, as 53.59% of those who left had overtime requirements, while single employees also represent the largest marital segment for attrition with 120 exits. From a role perspective, Laboratory Technicians and Sales Executives show high turnover volumes, often linked to education fields like Life Sciences and Medical. Lastly, the dashboard highlights that 65.82% of attrition occurs among those who travel rarely, suggesting that factors beyond travel frequency are at play. To address these issues, the report suggests monitoring overtime patterns, reviewing compensation for low-salary roles, and strengthening onboarding programs to improve first-year engagement
